It's Time For Learning!


Daytime is the period when sunlight illuminates a part of Earth, caused by the planet's rotation on its axis, which causes different parts to face the Sun. The Sun provides light and warmth, with its apparent path across the sky determining the time of day and causing the cycle of day and night.

The Moon appears in the night sky because it reflects sunlight, not because it produces its own light. While often associated with nighttime, the Moon is visible in the daytime sky about half the time. Its visibility during the night is influenced by its phases; a full moon is visible all night because it's opposite the Sun, while a new moon is closest to the Sun and therefore only visible during the day, or not at all.
